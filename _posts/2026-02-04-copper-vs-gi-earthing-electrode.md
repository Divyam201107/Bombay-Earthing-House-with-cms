One of the most common questions from engineers is whether to specify copper bonded or GI earthing electrodes. Both are standards-compliant but suit different applications, budgets and soil conditions.

## Conductivity

Copper has significantly lower electrical resistivity than galvanised iron, making it more efficient at conducting fault current. For critical installations — data centres, telecom exchanges, substations — copper bonded earth rods are typically preferred.

## Corrosion resistance

Copper bonded electrodes resist corrosion naturally and perform well even in acidic or saline soils. GI electrodes rely on their zinc coating; once that degrades, the underlying steel becomes vulnerable to rust and increased resistance.

## Cost

GI pipe electrodes are considerably more affordable than copper bonded rods, making them the standard choice for general residential, commercial and light industrial installations where soil conditions are not particularly aggressive.

## Lifespan

Copper bonded electrodes can deliver 20+ years of service with minimal degradation. GI electrodes typically offer 10–15 years depending on soil moisture and chemistry.

## Key takeaways

- Copper bonded offers superior conductivity and corrosion resistance — ideal for critical sites.
- GI pipe electrodes are cost-effective and code-compliant for general-purpose earthing.
- Soil testing should always inform the material decision.
